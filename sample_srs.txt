Healthcare EHR System — Software Requirements Specification (SRS)
Version 1.0 | Clinical Informatics Division

1. This document describes the vital signs monitoring module of the EHR system.
2. SpO2 is measured as a percentage of oxygen saturation in the blood.
3. The system shall alert the nurse when SpO2 drops below 90%.
4. The system shall alert the physician when heart rate exceeds 150 bpm.
5. A critical SpO2 alert must be displayed within 5 seconds of detection.
6. The system shall restrict access to patient records for unauthorized user roles.
7. Only physicians and nurses may acknowledge a critical alert.
8. The medication order workflow is managed according to NHS Digital standards.
9. A medication order shall transition from DRAFT to SUBMITTED only when all mandatory fields are completed.
10. The system shall prevent a CANCELLED medication order from being re-approved.
11. An e-prescription shall be verified by a pharmacist before dispensing.
12. Normal SpO2 range is 95% to 100% for healthy adults.
13. The system shall log all access attempts to patient records.
14. Blood pressure is measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg).
15. The system shall alert the nurse when systolic blood pressure exceeds 180 mmHg.
16. The system shall notify the physician if a critical alert is not acknowledged within 10 minutes.
17. Patients shall only have read-only access to their own health records.
18. The system shall display a drug interaction warning when two conflicting medications are ordered simultaneously.
19. Heart rate is measured in beats per minute.
20. The system shall generate a duplicate order alert if the same medication is prescribed twice within 24 hours.
21. Lab technicians shall not have access to prescribe or view medication orders.
22. The system shall require two-factor authentication for all physician logins.
23. CPOE stands for Computerized Physician Order Entry.
24. The system shall send an alert when respiratory rate falls below 8 breaths per minute.
25. All alert acknowledgements must be recorded with a timestamp and the acknowledging user's ID.
26. The system shall escalate an unacknowledged alert to the ward manager after 10 minutes.
27. Glucose levels below 50 mg/dL shall trigger an immediate critical alert to the treating physician.
28. The system shall prevent a medication order from transitioning from SUBMITTED to DISPENSED without APPROVED status.
29. A nurse shall not be able to prescribe medications, only administer them.
30. The system shall lock a user session after 15 minutes of inactivity and require re-authentication.
